## 1. The Studio Demo (the centerpiece)

A ~30–45 min live demo, end-to-end, on one small design problem so students see the *whole loop*, not isolated tricks.

**Demo arc (precedent → concept → form → representation → critique):**
1. **Precedent / research** — Use a chat model to gather and *interrogate* precedents. Show how to push past the first answer (ask for sources, contradictions, what's missing).
2. **Concept generation** — Text-to-image (Firefly / Midjourney / SDXL) to rapidly explore a design intent. Show prompt iteration: same idea, 5 framings.
3. **Form / massing** — Image-to-image or sketch-to-render to take a hand sketch into options.
4. **Representation** — Upscale, inpaint, expand a board; generate a diagram or section poché study.
5. **Critique loop** — Have the AI critique the scheme against the brief; then *you* critique the AI's critique. Model good skepticism.

> Teaching point of the demo: **AI is a fast collaborator, not an author.** Every output is a question, not an answer.

## 2. Where AI fits in the studio workflow

| Phase | AI use | Student keeps ownership of... |
|---|---|---|
| Research | Precedent synthesis, lit pull, glossary | Judgment of relevance & rigor |
| Concept | Image gen, mood/intent exploration | The design *thesis* |
| Iteration | Variations, sketch-to-render | Selection & reasoning |
| Drawing | Diagram drafts, board layout | Craft & accuracy |
| Writing | Brief/statement drafts, edits | Voice & argument |
| Critique | Devil's-advocate review | Final design decisions |

---

## 3. Candidate assignments

- **"Prompt to thesis"** — students generate 20 images, then write 1 page on *why* they kept 3. Grade the reasoning, not the renders.
- **AI precedent dossier** — use AI to assemble precedents, then fact-check & annotate every claim (catch the hallucinations).
- **Co-design log** — keep a transcript/log of every AI interaction in the project; submit it. Process visible.
- **"Break the AI"** — find where the tool fails on their site/program. Builds critical literacy.

---

## 4. Policy & integrity (decide before week 1)

- Disclosure expectation: log/cite AI use (which tool, what for).
- What's *encouraged* vs *not allowed* (e.g., final design rationale must be theirs).
- Equity: ensure free/low-cost tool options; nobody pays-to-win.
- Data/IP: don't upload others' copyrighted work or private info.
