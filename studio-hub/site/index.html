§1 · The idea in one paragraph

Design thinking is already AI-ready.

The bet of the studio.

Designers iterate, critique, prototype, and tolerate ambiguity — exactly the habits that working well with AI rewards. So the studio brings the design feedback loop to AI rather than treating it as an oracle. The through-lines: grade the trace, not the output; price trust against evidence; keep provenance; and keep the student — not the model — as the cognitive agent.

“Use AI to extend your reach, never to skip your reps.”

The assignment was never the product — you are the product; the assignment is the gym. Capability is rented; judgment is owned.

§3 · The spine

Six governing principles

These recur across every brainstorm document; together they form the course's posture toward the machine.

1Grade the trace, not the output

Assess the reasoning log and the AI interaction — transcript, refactor diff, failure museum — not just the final artifact.

2Interrogation is the method

The crit, pointed at the machine. “Make the strongest case that this is wrong” is the highest-value prompt.

3Calibrated skepticism

Trust is neither given nor withheld; it is priced, continuously, against evidence.

4Verification must be external

The model cannot be the court that acquits itself. Triangulate; run the code; check every citation.

5You stay the cognitive agent

AI is argued with, wielded, checked, or constrained — never the second brain.

6Provenance — the log

Four lines for any AI-touched work: tool · asked · kept-changed-rejected · verified. Disclosure as process, not detection.

§4 · The bridge

The architect's apparatus maps onto AI work

Architecture's 600-year-old delegation machinery already is the skillset for directing AI.

RFI (request for information)The agent's clarifying questions.
Shop-drawing reviewReading the diff — review for conformance to intent; don't redo.
Spec / dimension string / detailA natural-language program with tolerances.
Literal-but-wrong contractorThe model's native failure mode: fluent, compliant, wrong.

The studio loop is the agent loop: propose → act → observe → revise = scheme → pin-up → crit → revision.
