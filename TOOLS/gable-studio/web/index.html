Where the analysis comes from

These are transparent teaching proxies, not validated simulation. Each metric is a pure, deterministic function of the parameters — the same formula runs in the browser and in the exported Rhino/Grasshopper python (a parity test checks they agree). For certified analysis use Ladybug Tools / EnergyPlus (energy), Radiance (daylight), or CFD (wind).

Frame & sun position

+X East, +Y North, +Z Up; azimuth clockwise from North. Sun is sampled at 3 declinations (δ = +23.45° summer, 0° equinox, −23.45° winter) × 5 hours (08–16). Altitude: sin α = sin φ·sin δ + cos φ·cos δ·cos H; azimuth: cos A = (sin δ − sin φ·sin α)/(cos φ·cos α), with hour angle H = 15°·(hour−12). Standard solar geometry — Duffie & Beckman, “Solar Engineering of Thermal Processes”; NOAA solar-position equations.

Solar gain idx

Direct-beam incidence proxy: Σ area · max(0, n·L) over the sun samples (Lambert cosine law), summed over the whole envelope and, separately, over the glazed apertures (“useful” gain), split by season and orientation. Ignores diffuse/cloud, glazing transmittance, and self-shading — so read it as relative, not W.

Wind exposure & load m² · N

Windward projected area Σ A · max(0, n·f) (f = wind-from direction). Load proxy = dynamic pressure q = ½·ρ·V² (ρ = 1.225 kg/m³) × exposure. ASHRAE / Eurocode EN 1991-1-4 dynamic pressure; true pressure coefficients need wind-tunnel/CFD.

Wind channelling ×

Plan “throat” ratio between the walls and the plinth across the wind (continuity/venturi heuristic): wider building ÷ narrowest side gap. A massing cue, not CFD.

Stack ventilation idx · m

Buoyancy-driven flow Q = Cd · A* · √(2g·Δz·ΔT/T), effective opening area A* = 1/√(1/A_in² + 1/A_out²), with low apertures as inlets and high ones (incl. the roof) as outlets; Cd = 0.61, T = 293 K. CIBSE AM10 “Natural ventilation in non-domestic buildings”; ASHRAE Fundamentals.

Views sr

Projected solid angle of each aperture from an interior eye point, Ω ≈ A·cos θ / r²; “view toward target” weights Ω by alignment to a chosen azimuth; “sky view” uses the roof aperture upward. Solid-angle / view-factor geometry.

Earth coupling & thermal mass m² · MJ/K·m³

Soil-contact area = plinth faces below the terrain height; thermal-mass ratio = (slab volume × ≈2.0 MJ/m³·K for concrete) ÷ enclosed volume; buried fraction = slab side below grade ÷ thickness. Concrete volumetric heat capacity ≈ 2.0 MJ/m³·K, CIBSE Guide A; steady-geometry proxy, not transient heat-flow.

Terrain

The ravine is procedural: a smoothstep drop across an edge plus gentle sinusoidal undulation (Perlin smoothstep t²(3−2t)) — illustrative ground, not survey data.
