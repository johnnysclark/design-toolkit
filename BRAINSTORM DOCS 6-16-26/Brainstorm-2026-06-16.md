## General thoughts (the pedagogy)

- **Grade the trace, not the output.** Assess the process/log of reasoning + AI interaction, not the final artifact.
- **Vibe-code a tool together** vs. **give them a tool to use** — two different assignment modes; decide per project.
- **Divergent vs. convergent thinking** — where does AI help generate options vs. narrow them? Don't let it collapse divergence too early.
- **Cognitive offloading** — teach it explicitly, or use AI to *demonstrate* what offloading does to thinking.
- **Socratic Mentor workflow** — AI that asks rather than answers. Possible default mode for the studio.
- **Intrinsic motivation** — anything built must *bolster* it, not replace the satisfaction of designing.
- **Skill trade-offs** — name what skills get traded away by spending time on AI. Make the cost visible.
- **Steelman** — have students build the strongest argument *for* a specific AI use (and against).
- **Anti-Pinterest** — counter to passive scroll/collect image-culture; AI as active reasoning, not mood-board mining.

---

## Tensions worth designing around

- *Build vs. use:* building teaches how AI works + agency; using teaches judgment + speed. Probably want both, sequenced.
- *Offload vs. develop:* every offload is a skill not practiced. Which skills are core (must develop) vs. peripheral (ok to offload)?
- *Divergent vs. convergent:* AI is great at volume (divergent) but seductive at premature convergence. Anti-Pinterest lives here.
- *Trace vs. output:* grading the trace rewards thinking but is harder to assess at scale — need a rubric.
